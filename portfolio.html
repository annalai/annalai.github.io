Alternative Energy Systems

For ME141: Alternative Energy Systems, our team was presented with the challenge of designing a thermal management system for a RC car powered by 3-cell LiFePO4 battery pack. Weights were carried up a hill using the car until the battery powering the car had drained, overheated to 55℃, or 10 minutes had elapsed. Our design incorporated active and passive cooling through use of fans, heat sinks, thermal paste, and Peltier coolers which kept the battery below 30℃ during operation.

Jumping Robot

The final project of ME112: Mechanical Systems Design challenged our team to create a battery-powered jumping robot able to jump one meter and stick to a Velcro ceiling. The robot is inspired by Mynocks, the galactic pests that cling onto and consume the electricity of spaceships. The Mynock’s jumping movements mimic those of the earth frog, which serves as biomimetic inspiration. Read the full design report here.

Hua Bottle Opener

A bottle opener inspired by the story of Hua Mulan. Machined in 316 stainless steel, the bottle opener captures the strength of Mulan in contrast to the delicate Mongolia (木兰) flower her name represents. After becoming a war hero, Mulan chooses to return to her family. To reflect this I chose to use her family name, Hua (花), which naturally evolves into the head of the bottle opener and touches on how the story’s interpretation has changed overtime – from one of fulfilling filial duties to that of empowerment.

Droplets

Undergraduate research conducted in Prakash Lab, with the BioX USRP and BioE REU, studying self-assembling vapor-mediated droplets.

I developed large-scale droplet deposition devices using machine shop tools to investigate lattice systems of droplets, fabricated microfluidic devices, and aided in the design of optical setups using Arduinos. Feel free to contact me for more information.
